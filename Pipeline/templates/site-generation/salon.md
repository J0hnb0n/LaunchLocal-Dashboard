### Salon / Spa / Personal Services Requirements

(Applies to: hair salons, barbershops, nail salons, estheticians, massage, med-spa, lash/brow studios, tanning, tattoo/piercing studios.)

**Hero section:**
- Photography-driven: styled hair, salon interior, or a confident portrait shot. Avoid generic "woman touching her face" stock imagery.
- Headline evokes experience and expertise ("Balayage + color craft in downtown Barrie" beats "Beautiful Hair Awaits").
- Primary CTA: **Book Now** — link to their booking platform if they have one (Vagaro, Booksy, Fresha, Square Appointments, Mindbody), otherwise prominent phone CTA.

**Required sections:**
1. **Services & pricing** — Grouped menu with price ranges (e.g., "Haircut & style — from $65"). Pricing ranges are fine; hard prices aren't required for salons where the service varies. If client hasn't provided, use a reasonable industry template and mark `<!-- PLACEHOLDER PRICING: confirm with client -->`.
2. **Booking integration** — If known booking platform, embed or deep-link to it. A visible "Book Now" button should persist across the site (sticky header on mobile).
3. **Team / stylists** — Grid of stylist/staff photos with names, specialties, and individual booking links if supported. Humanizes the business and supports rebooking relationships.
4. **Gallery** — High-quality photos of work (hair transformations, nail art, spa interiors). Instagram-style responsive grid, lazy-loaded. This is the most important persuasion tool for this category.
5. **About / philosophy** — What makes this salon different: product lines used (Aveda, Davines, Oribe, clean beauty, etc.), technique specialties, atmosphere, client demographic they serve best.
6. **Hours** — Prominent, with special note if appointments-only vs. walk-ins welcome.
7. **Location** — Embedded map, address, parking notes.

**Optional but high-value:**
- **Gift cards** — direct purchase or inquiry CTA
- **New client special / first-visit offer** — a specific promotional callout
- **Product sales** if they retail (small shop section)
- **Instagram feed embed** — if they have an active, well-curated account
- **Membership / packages** for med-spa and regular-service businesses

**Industry-specific SEO:**
- Structured data type: `BeautySalon`, `HairSalon`, `NailSalon`, `DaySpa`, `HealthAndBeautyBusiness` — use most specific.
- Include `priceRange` and service areas.
- Title format: `{Business Name} — {Primary Service} in {City}`

**Design cues:**
- Refined and on-brand for their aesthetic — a high-end balayage salon looks very different from a neighborhood barbershop. Match the vibe they signal on their Instagram or storefront.
- Softer/elevated typography and generous whitespace for beauty/spa. Bolder, grittier for barbershops.
- Color palettes often muted/sophisticated — rose/cream/charcoal for beauty, black/oxblood/gold for barbershops. Let the client's colors lead.
- Large, beautiful imagery is non-negotiable. A salon site with small or mediocre photos loses credibility instantly.
