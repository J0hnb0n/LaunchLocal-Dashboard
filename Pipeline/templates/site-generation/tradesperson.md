### Tradesperson-Specific Requirements

(Applies to: plumbers, electricians, HVAC, roofers, contractors, landscapers, handymen, painters, locksmiths, etc.)

**Hero section:**
- Headline targets the customer's problem or moment of need ("24/7 Emergency Plumbing in Barrie" beats "Quality Plumbing Services").
- Include the **service area** prominently ("Serving {city} and surrounding areas within 50km").
- Primary CTA: **Call Now** with the phone number visible (not just a button) — tradespeople convert on phone calls, not forms.
- Secondary CTA: **Request a Quote** (form) for non-emergency work.

**Required sections:**
1. **Services** — Grid of service categories with clear icons/photos. Each service links to a section or dedicated page describing it. Tell the customer *exactly* what's included.
2. **Trust signals bar** — Licensed, insured, bonded, WSIB, years in business, certifications (Red Seal, Gas Technician license, etc.), BBB rating, Google review stars. Display as a horizontal band of badges near the top. **Never invent credentials** — leave `<!-- PLACEHOLDER: confirm client's actual certifications -->` if unknown.
3. **Service area** — Named list of cities/neighborhoods served, or an embedded map with a service radius highlighted. This is critical for local SEO and for customer confidence.
4. **Why choose us** — 3-6 short cards: upfront pricing, on-time arrival, clean workmanship, warranty/guarantee, locally-owned, same-day service. Be specific — "We arrive within the booked time window 98% of the time" beats "Reliable service".
5. **Before/after gallery** — Project photos if available. Shows real work, builds credibility. Use lazy loading. Leave placeholder grid if client hasn't provided.
6. **Testimonials** — Pull 3-5 Google review quotes if the client has a strong Google profile. Include reviewer first name + last initial. **Never fabricate testimonials** — leave clearly commented placeholders.
7. **Emergency / after-hours** block if applicable — separate phone number or highlighted box.
8. **Contact / Quote form** — Name, phone (required), email, service type (dropdown), brief description, preferred contact method. Short form — trades leads are often mobile, one-thumb.

**Optional but high-value:**
- **Financing / payment plans** if offered
- **Maintenance plans / service agreements** for HVAC/plumbing
- **FAQ** addressing common cost/process questions — strong SEO value

**Industry-specific SEO:**
- Structured data type: `LocalBusiness` or specific (`Plumber`, `Electrician`, `HVACBusiness`, `RoofingContractor`, etc.) — use the most specific available.
- Include `areaServed` as a list of cities/regions.
- Title format: `{Service} in {City} — {Business Name}`
- Content should naturally include "{service} near me" intent phrasing.

**Design cues:**
- Professional and trustworthy over flashy. Think work-truck aesthetic done well — bold, confident, clean.
- High contrast, strong CTAs, phone number always visible in header (sticky on mobile).
- Safety colors (blue, red, yellow) work well and match many existing trade brands, but don't override a client's established colors.
- Avoid cutesy icons or playful typography — customers want competence signals.
