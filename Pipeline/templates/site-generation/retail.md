### Retail / Small Shop Requirements

(Applies to: independent shops, boutiques, bookstores, florists, gift shops, specialty food, hobby/craft shops. For shops that need full e-commerce, flag it — this template targets brick-and-mortar with a web presence, not full Shopify-style storefronts.)

**Hero section:**
- Lead with a photograph that conveys the shop experience — the storefront, a curated display, a shopper browsing. Skip generic "shopping bag" imagery.
- Headline establishes the shop's identity or niche ("Independent bookshop, Collingwood's best curated fiction" beats "Books & More").
- Primary CTA depends on the business: **Visit Us** (with address/hours), **Shop Online** (if they have e-commerce), or **Call to Hold an Item** for specialty shops.

**Required sections:**
1. **Featured products / collections** — 4-12 items in a visual grid. Photo, name, short description, price. If they have e-commerce, link to product pages; otherwise these showcase what's in-store. Rotatable / seasonal.
2. **About / story** — Why this shop exists, what they curate for, who the owner is. Small retail lives on personal connection — this section matters more than in other industries.
3. **Visit us** — Address, hours, parking, embedded map. Include photos of the storefront and interior if available.
4. **Shop categories or departments** — Clear entry points into whatever they sell, even if not shoppable online. Signal to visitors that it's worth the drive.
5. **Hours** — Very prominent. Small retail is hours-sensitive — customers check before driving over. Call out holiday/seasonal hours.
6. **Contact** — Phone, email, and a simple question form (e.g., "Looking for something specific? Ask us.").

**Optional but high-value:**
- **Events & workshops** — book signings, craft nights, tastings, classes — retail drivers of foot traffic
- **Newsletter signup** — primary email list for sales/events. Integrate Mailchimp/ConvertKit or mark as TODO in README.
- **Loyalty / rewards** program info if they have one
- **Gift cards** CTA
- **Buy local / community story** — for indie shops, community positioning matters
- **Instagram feed embed** — if they're active on social
- **Delivery / local pickup** info if applicable

**Industry-specific SEO:**
- Structured data type: `Store` or specific (`BookStore`, `Florist`, `ClothingStore`, `ToyStore`, etc.) — use most specific.
- Include `paymentAccepted` and `hasMap`.
- Title format: `{Business Name} — {Shop Type} in {City}`
- Emphasize "shop local" and neighborhood positioning in copy.

**Design cues:**
- Reflect the shop's personality — a rare bookstore should feel different from a candle shop. Match the vibe of their physical space.
- Clean, editorial layouts often work well — let product/space photography carry the design.
- Avoid loud e-commerce patterns (big "SALE" banners, countdown timers) unless that's genuinely their brand.
- Typography can be more characterful here than in services categories — a bookshop might use a serif display font; a modern gift shop might use a geometric sans.
- If they have strong brand colors (signage, awnings, packaging), build the site palette around them.
